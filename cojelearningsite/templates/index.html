Welcome

Johannesburgs public libraries are hubs of education, giving residents access to books, magazines and audio-visual material. Besides lending out material, the City's libraries offer a wide range of services including literacy classes, storytelling, sessions for youngsters, classroom support, gatherings for older residents and literary competitions. They are also the venues for lectures, exhibitions and talks. Libraries can be found accross the City's seven regions, and mobile satelite centres also travel to far-flung areas.

All residents are welcome to join their local library. You need to present your identity book, with proof of residence. Children will also need to show their birth certificates. Cards will be issued by the library. Those applying for the first time will have to wait 5 working days for their documents to be processed, membership is free

Membership

All residents are welcome to join their local library. You need to present your identity book along with your proof of residence. Children who wish to join have to present their birth certificates. Upon being accepted, the library will issue the user with a library card. The general waiting period for a member to be processed is five working days. If you wish to take morethan five books at a time a membership fee of R30 will apply. Find out more by contacting or visiting your local library

Conditions of use

The owner of the card is responsible for all the material that awre issued on the card. The card expires after 3 years. If you still have the card it is for free to renew your membership. If you lose your card a fine must be paid. You will be held liable for any lost books and you will be charged the full price of the said book. You are liable for the upkeep of books that you have borrowed and the return of these books on time. No material can be borrowed until the fine is settled. Treat the library building, corner collections as well as other library users with the upmost respect.
